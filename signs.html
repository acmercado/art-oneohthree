phase 1: "sign series"

title:

Every Day Signs

date:

10/27/2020

medium:

JPG Imgs, Adobe's PhotoShop and Illustrator

map of signs

the red dots correspond to the order of the signs shown from right to left.

description/process:

for this phase, I wanted to find places that a "covid-19" related sign would be placed but instead I ended up going to areas that not only relate to myself, but possibly other people. I used Illustrator to make my signs and PhotoShop to make it seem as if they were actually in the place the pictures were taken.

. . .

the first sign is at the "Parma Sister City Park" and it's one of the many parks in my neighborhood that had their basketball hoops/backboards removed in order to prevent the spread of COVID-19.

. . .

the second place already has an existing sign but I changed it to be more relevant to the drivers of today.

. . .

the third destination is technically a "road ends here" area, but many people (teens and young adults) come here to cruise around on their skateboards, longboards, bikes, and roller skates for fun.

. . .

the fourth area has a sign that showcases what could happen if one didn't slow down before encountering the "speed bump".

live link:

Fusion 360 - Cat Block

simulation .gifs

description/process:

for my first block, i chose the letter "a" since it's the first letter of my name. i was playing around with different fonts, but the drill bit size wouldn't have been able to get inside the "nooks and crannies" of the font style details.

. . .

i first created a sketch of a 4" x 5" rectangle, modified it to have an offset of .25", inserted my .svg file from illustrator, then extruded the parts i wanted to be at .75" depth and .64" depth.

. . .

for the second block, i decided to make a cat with (more like a third) of its face as a skull because i wanted to have a cat block to represent my two cats, momo and tora, but also didn't want it to be too plain (thus the skull side). as for the process, i did the same things as the first one. the only difference is that some of the shapes from my .svg file got somewhat distorted so i ended up just deleting them and fixing them in the sketch of fusion 360.

artist statement/thoughts:

these block designs were kind of confusing at first, mostly because of trying to export my illustrator file into an .svg file. i found it was best to change the settings of the svg options for font to "convert to outlines". the rest of the process to make the blocks was pretty easy considering the fusion 360 exercises we have done already. when it came to the manufacturing part, i got a bit lost because there was a bunch of numbers being thrown at me but after re-watching the zoom recording, i came to understand the process more. it was also really cool to see the entire thing be simulated! :D
