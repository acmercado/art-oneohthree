How is This a "Cultural Artifact" ?

Personally, I consider the Gaming community a culture in which people who enjoy playing games, making/designing games, and critiquing games can all come together. We can enjoy games together through multiplayer games or simply converse about similar interests or dislikes about certain games. We can create relationships, fan art, cosplays, and other games within this community.

. . .

The Wii remote is an artifact as it is part of the Gaming community, specfically belonging to Nintendo's Wii console (which was released in 2006). The Wii remote a wireless controller with a power button, a D-Pad, an "A" button, "1" and "2" buttons, a Trigger button, a plus and minus button, and a Home button. It also has a sensor that controls the movement of the user's cursor on whichever monitor it is used on, a built-in speaker, and LEDs to determine which player number the user is.

. . .

Today, the Wii remote is replaced by Nintendo's newest console: The Switch (with their controllers called "Joy-Cons"). The Joy-con is often a pair of two controllers that could also be used individually. Nintendo revisits their old GameCube controllers in which they return the joysticks, the "XYAB" buttons, and L1/R1 and L2/R2 triggers. They can be used, connected to the console or not. Compared to the Joy-Con, the Wii remote seems like it was ahead of its time. I can also say that the new generation today doesn't even know what a Wii remote (or what a Wii console) is.
