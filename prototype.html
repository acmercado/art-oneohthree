"the plan + enclosure (???)"

from the photos above, i was then thinking i could use the ps4 controller as an enclosure in which the top square would hold the display, and the analog joysticks would be placed in their "original" homes. i would also use the (now open) holes to feed wires through and possibly have a separate (small) box to contain the boards if they cannot fit within the controller itself.

. . .

in addition, i was also thinking of replacing the shape buttons with momentary buttons, and the d-pad with neopixel leds (below shows a more flushed out illustration)

"the purpose (?)"

in general, i want to see how the joycons, buttons, leds, and gyro perform on the display. i also want to see what the gyro can do in general and how it may "distort" whatever is being shown on screen.

. . .

the intended outcome for what i hope it may result in is having the joysticks control movement of a pointer on screen (similar to an etch-a-sketch). the buttons would then allow different things to occur on the screen such as make shapes appear or change colors of the drawing. as for the leds, they would light up randomly according to which buttons are pressed. in addition, the gyro hardware would controll the perspective of the drawing (or even just possibly shift or erase the drawing).

. . .

overall, i want the user to be able to experiment on his/her own, making a "test" drawing to see what each thing does, then create a new drawing to express how it wants to interact with the "machine." the led buttons on the other hand, will show how the "machine" feels of the drawing and how it's being handled. i want it to be on a ps4 controller to insinuate the feeling of the user playing a game.
