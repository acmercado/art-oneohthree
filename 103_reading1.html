"reading 1"

"What is a good working definition of what a System is and perhaps what it is not?"

Personally, I believe a great definition of a System is multiple components that work together to create a network while also becoming one whole. A definition of what a System is not would be single individual working parts.

"Modularity"

The idea that the pieces of a whole can be rearranged to create new and different things. An example of modularity could be the Nerf Gun Modulus series; one can connect different types of guns and accessories to one specific gun to create a unique, massive weaponry toy.

"Decomposability"

The ability to have something be broken down into smaller parts but will reach a point where it can no longer do so. For example, continuously cutting paper until it becomes a "speck" and is now unusable to be cut down even more or be used for other the many other purposes paper has to offer.

"Emergence"

The idea of “new” forming out of complex systems. An example of this could be a the formation of snowflakes or the creation of snow piles themselves.

"Chaos Theory"

The idea that there are infinite outcomes (whether they are good or bad) after one makes a decision, or performs an act, which may affect the environment around the subject. For example, going down the "rabbit hole" of the YouTube algorithm if one's "autoplay" has been turned on and is allowed to keep on going.

"Give us your definition of what Tesselation means and give an example you encountered, not mentioned in the reading."

Tessellation is the creation of patterns, either in a symmetrical or asymmetrical form, whilst using math or a design that could be repeated. An example of tessellation that I have encountered are in art works/crafts (in mosaics and making snowflakes out of paper) and in some people's rugs.

"What is the difference between ideas of Modularity and Tesselation? What are the properties that are in opposition with each other?"

The difference between the terms “Tessellation” and “Modulation” is that tessellations can be rearranged but are limited to a certain amount of outcomes whereas modulations may be rearranged to create an infinite amount of outcomes.

"What is the difference between designing something that has Complexity (aperiodic) vs being Uniform (periodic)?"

Complexity (aperiodic) designs have irregular patterns and may be categorized into three types of patterns such as Penrose, Pinwheel, and Wang tiling. They may often be associated with organic shapes/forms and be unpredictable. On the other hand, uniform (periodic) designs have regular patterns with no “disturbances,” are associated with geometric shapes/forms, and are predictable.
